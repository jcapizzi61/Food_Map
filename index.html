Where are New Yorkers Facing Food Deserts?

As grocery prices and rent rise in certain neighborhoods, some New York families struggle to find affordable food

By Julia Capizzi

December 19, 2021

While inflation continues and economic recovery from the pandemic remains uncertain, food prices are rising at unprecedented rates all over the world. But for low income New Yorkers, gentrification is also making it more difficult to buy the food they need.

“There are certain neighborhoods that are more expensive than others. For example, Chelsea, where there are still pockets of very low income New Yorkers living in public housing who are at a disadvantage because their neighborhood stores are priced for the more affluent residents,” said Nevin Cohen, associate professor at the City University of New York School of Public Health.

Food deserts, neighborhoods where access to affordable grocery stores is limited, are often a direct result of gentrification. While one might expect that more densely populated areas would also have more grocery stores, that’s not always the case.
